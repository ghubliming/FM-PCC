# FIX_12_P2: Strict Parity & Scaling Bridge - Summary of Changes

## 1. Data Normalization (The Scaling Bridge)
*   **Module**: Created `ddpm_encdec_vision/utils/scaler.py`.
*   **Logic**: Before training, the script scans the `train_files.pkl` dataset to calculate the Global Mean and Standard Deviation of robot positions and actions.
*   **Impact**: Maps raw meters (e.g., 0.005m) to a standardized range (Mean 0, Std 1). This matches the numerical power of the Diffusion Noise ($\mathcal{N}(0, 1)$), allowing the U-Net to "see" the trajectory details.
*   **Files Modified**: 
    *   `train_ddpm_encdec_vision.py` (Initialized and saved scaler).
    *   `ddpm_encdec_vision/utils/training.py` (Injected scaler into the `Trainer` batch loop).

## 2. Mixed-Loop Evaluation (The "Mental Map")
*   **Logic**: Implemented a "Mental Map" for robot proprioception.
    *   **Vision (Closed-Loop)**: The model continues to look at fresh camera images at every re-planning step to find the box.
    *   **State (Open-Loop)**: The model is conditioned on its own **Planned Position** (`mental_robot_pos`), not the measured simulation coordinates.
*   **Impact**: Prevents "Jitter Feedthrough." Even if the simulation drifts due to IK errors ($8e-06$), the model remains stable because it trusts its own internal path more than the noisy simulator data.
*   **Files Modified**: 
    *   `eval_ddpm_encdec_vision.py` (Updated `VisualAgentWrapper` to maintain and update the `mental_robot_pos`).

## 3. Preservation of Gen5 "Brain"
*   **MPC Integration**: The Horizon-8/10 U-Net trajectory logic was preserved. The model still plans an 8-step "Foresight" trajectory, providing smoother motion than point-by-point ACT.
*   **Hyperparameters**: No changes were made to Learning Rate (`1e-4`) or Batch Size (`8`) as per user request.

## 4. Diagnostic Observability (FIX_12 Synergy)
*   The `rollout_X_report.png` generated by FIX_12 will now reflect the **Inverse-Scaled** trajectories.
*   The **Z-Height** will now be learned and scaled correctly, preventing the "floating robot" failure mode.
